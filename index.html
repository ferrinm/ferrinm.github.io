in vivo approach

  • trial experiments on different microscopes indicate our airyscan should work well for 3D quantitative characterization of condensates over time
    • typically 5-10 s per ~ 30μm X 30μm X 10 μm volume, single channel
    • minimal photobleaching even after ~10 minutes continuous imaging

  • example 3D max projection

    • merging events seem to primarily come from actin-driven splitting and then snapping back; I'm nervous about treating these like passive merging events
  • another example from airyscan
  • for comparison, spinning disk takes longer to image the same volume and much more severe photobleaching
  • next I will explore imaging LatA-treated cells to see if that yields more passive condensate fusion events
